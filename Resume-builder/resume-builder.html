Key Sections of a Resume

Contact Information

The top section should include:

  • Full name
  • Phone number
  • Professional email address
  • LinkedIn profile
  • Portfolio website (if applicable)

Avoid including unnecessary personal information such as marital status, religion, or age.

Professional Summary

A professional summary is a short introduction that highlights your experience, skills, and career goals.

Example:

Results-driven software engineer with 4+ years of experience developing scalable web applications using React, JavaScript, and cloud technologies. Proven ability to improve system performance and deliver high-quality solutions.

A strong summary immediately communicates your value to employers.

Work Experience

Your work experience section should focus on achievements rather than responsibilities.

Instead of:

❌ Responsible for managing projects.

Write:

✅ Managed 15+ client projects, achieving 98% on-time delivery.

Include:

  • Job title
  • Company name
  • Employment dates
  • Key achievements
  • Measurable results

Education

Include:

  • Degree
  • Institution name
  • Graduation year
  • Academic achievements

Example:

Bachelor of Technology in Computer Science

ABC University  |  2024  |  CGPA: 8.5/10

Skills

Skills help recruiters quickly identify your qualifications.

Examples of technical skills:

  • Python
  • Java
  • SQL
  • React
  • Excel
  • Power BI

Examples of soft skills:

  • Leadership
  • Communication
  • Teamwork
  • Problem-solving
  • Time management

Certifications

Professional certifications strengthen your profile. Examples:

  • Google Data Analytics Certificate
  • AWS Certified Cloud Practitioner
  • PMP Certification
  • Microsoft Azure Fundamentals

Projects

Projects are especially important for students and freshers. Include:

  • Project title
  • Technologies used
  • Problem solved
  • Key outcomes

Projects demonstrate practical skills even when professional experience is limited.

What Makes a Resume ATS-Friendly?

An Applicant Tracking System scans resumes before they reach recruiters. ATS-friendly resumes typically:

  • Use standard section headings
  • Include relevant keywords
  • Avoid complex graphics
  • Use simple formatting
  • Maintain consistent structure

Density Resume Builder automatically follows ATS best practices.

Resume Writing Tips

Tailor Your Resume

Customize your resume for each job application.

Use Action Verbs

Examples:

  • Developed
  • Implemented
  • Managed
  • Designed
  • Led
  • Increased

Quantify Achievements

Numbers make achievements more impactful.

Example: "Increased website traffic by 45% in six months."

Keep It Concise

Most resumes should be one page for freshers and two pages for experienced professionals.

Proofread Carefully

Grammar and spelling mistakes can hurt your credibility.

Common Resume Mistakes to Avoid

Using Generic Objectives

Avoid vague statements that do not communicate value.

Including Irrelevant Information

Focus only on information related to the target role.

Poor Formatting

Maintain consistency throughout your resume.

Keyword Stuffing

Use relevant keywords naturally instead of overloading the resume.

Ignoring ATS Requirements

Complex designs can sometimes cause ATS parsing issues.

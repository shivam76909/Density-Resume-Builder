What Is an ATS?

ATS stands for Applicant Tracking System. It is software used by employers to collect, organize, and filter job applications.

Instead of manually reviewing hundreds of resumes, employers use ATS software to identify candidates who match the job requirements.

Popular ATS Platforms include:

  • Workday
  • Greenhouse
  • Lever
  • BambooHR
  • iCIMS
  • Taleo
  • SmartRecruiters
  • Jobvite

Why ATS Matters

Many companies receive hundreds or even thousands of applications for a single position.

ATS software helps recruiters:

  • Filter applications quickly
  • Search resumes using keywords
  • Rank candidates
  • Track hiring progress
  • Manage recruitment workflows

If your resume is not ATS-compatible, it may be rejected before a recruiter sees it.

How ATS Software Works

The ATS scans your resume and extracts key information such as:

  • Name
  • Contact Information
  • Skills
  • Work Experience
  • Education
  • Certifications
  • Keywords

The system then compares your resume against the job description. Resumes with stronger keyword matches are usually ranked higher.

What Is an ATS Resume Format?

An ATS Resume Format is a resume structure designed to be easily read and processed by applicant tracking systems.

ATS-friendly resumes feature:

  • Standard section headings
  • Simple formatting
  • Relevant keywords
  • Clean layouts
  • Professional fonts
  • Clear work experience descriptions

The goal is to ensure the software can accurately interpret your information.

ATS Resume Format Rules

Use Standard Headings

Always use common section names that software easily parses: Professional Summary, Work Experience, Skills, Education, Certifications, and Projects.

❌ My Career Journey

✔ Work Experience

Use Reverse Chronological Format

ATS systems and recruiters generally prefer listing your most recent job first, followed by older jobs. This makes your career progression easy to understand.

Keep Formatting Simple

Use:

✔ Bullet points

✔ Clear headings

✔ Consistent spacing

✔ Standard fonts

Avoid:

❌ Text boxes

❌ Graphics & images

❌ Tables & charts

❌ Icons

Many ATS systems cannot properly parse these non-text elements and may scrambling your content.

ATS-Friendly Fonts

Use clean, professional fonts: Arial, Calibri, Helvetica, Times New Roman, Georgia.

Recommended font size: Headings: 12–14 pt | Body Text: 10–12 pt

ATS Keywords: Why They Matter

Keywords are one of the most important ATS ranking factors. The software searches for terms related to: Skills, Job titles, Certifications, Technologies, and Responsibilities.

Example: If the job description includes Project Management, Agile, Scrum, and Stakeholder Management, your resume should naturally include those exact terms if they match your experience.

How to Find ATS Keywords

Review the job description and identify:

  • Skills: e.g., Python, SEO, Salesforce, Data Analysis
  • Certifications: e.g., PMP, AWS Certified Solutions Architect, Google Analytics Certification
  • Responsibilities: e.g., Team Leadership, Project Planning, Customer Relationship Management

Include relevant keywords naturally throughout your professional summary, skills, and work history sections.

ATS Resume Format for Freshers

Freshers often have limited work experience. Focus on Education, Academic Projects, Internships, Certifications, and Skills.

Recommended Structure:

  • Contact Information
  • Career Objective
  • Education
  • Skills
  • Projects
  • Internships
  • Certifications

Example Career Objective

Motivated Computer Science graduate with strong programming and analytical skills. Seeking an entry-level Software Developer position to apply technical knowledge and contribute to innovative projects.

ATS Resume Format for Experienced Professionals

Experienced professionals should prioritize Work Experience, Achievements, Skills, and Certifications.

Example Professional Summary

Results-driven Product Manager with 7+ years of experience leading product development and driving business growth. Proven track record of managing cross-functional teams, launching successful products, and improving customer engagement.

ATS Resume Template Structure

The ideal, safest ATS-friendly resume structure is:

  1. Contact Information
  2. Professional Summary
  3. Skills
  4. Work Experience
  5. Education
  6. Certifications
  7. Projects / Achievements (Optional)

This layout functions optimally across almost all industries and ATS platforms.

ATS Resume Checklist

Before submitting your job application, verify your resume matches the following requirements:

✔ Uses standard headings
✔ Includes relevant keywords
✔ Uses reverse chronological format
✔ Has no tables or text boxes
✔ Uses professional fonts
✔ Includes measurable achievements
✔ Has no spelling or grammar errors
✔ Saved as PDF or DOCX

Common ATS Resume Mistakes

  • Keyword Stuffing: Adding excessive keywords in blocks or invisible text can trigger spam filters. Use keywords naturally in context.
  • Using Graphics: Images, icons, and diagrams cannot be parsed, and might block subsequent text from being read. Keep it strictly text-based.
  • Complex Formatting: Avoid custom columns, tables, headers, and footers since many older ATS parsers read left-to-right across columns.
  • Missing Keywords: Many candidates fail automated screening simply because they fail to customize the skills keywords from the job listing.
  • Unclear Job Titles: Use recognizable, standard job titles.

✔ Standard: Digital Marketing Executive

❌ Confusing: Online Growth Ninja

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about parsing algorithms and ATS optimization.

What is an ATS resume?+
An ATS resume is a resume designed to be easily read and processed by Applicant Tracking Systems.
What resume format is best for ATS?+
The Reverse Chronological Resume Format is generally considered the most ATS-friendly because it clearly timelines your experience.
Can ATS read PDFs?+
Most modern ATS systems can read PDFs, but some older systems prefer DOCX. Always check the file format instructions on the application page.
Do ATS systems reject resumes automatically?+
Yes. ATS software can automatically filter out or flag as low match resumes that lack relevant keywords or fail formatting parsing checks.
How important are keywords?+
Keywords are extremely important because they are the main search criteria recruiters use to query their candidate databases.
Is ATS optimization necessary?+
Yes. Over 90% of large employers and Fortune 500 companies use ATS tools to handle recruiting.

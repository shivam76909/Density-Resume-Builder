What Is a Cover Letter?

A cover letter is a one-page document submitted along with your resume when applying for jobs.

Its purpose is to:

  • Introduce yourself
  • Explain your interest in the role
  • Highlight relevant qualifications
  • Demonstrate enthusiasm
  • Show why you're a strong candidate

A well-written cover letter can help you stand out from other applicants.

Why Is a Cover Letter Important?

Many job seekers skip cover letters, but recruiters often use them to evaluate:

  • Communication skills
  • Motivation
  • Professionalism
  • Cultural fit
  • Interest in the company

A strong cover letter provides context that your resume alone cannot communicate.

How to Write a Cover Letter

A professional cover letter typically contains four sections.

1. Header

Include:

  • Full Name
  • Phone Number
  • Email Address
  • LinkedIn Profile
  • Date

2. Opening Paragraph

Introduce yourself and explain the position you're applying for.

Example:

Dear Hiring Manager,

I am excited to apply for the Software Engineer position at ABC Technologies. With strong experience in web development and problem-solving, I am confident in my ability to contribute to your team.

3. Body Paragraph

Highlight your qualifications and achievements.

Example:

In my previous role, I developed scalable web applications using React and Node.js, helping improve application performance by 35%. I enjoy collaborating with teams to build innovative solutions that deliver business value.

4. Closing Paragraph

End with enthusiasm and a call to action.

Example:

Thank you for considering my application. I would welcome the opportunity to discuss how my skills and experience can contribute to your organization.

Cover Letter Writing Tips

Customize Every Application

Avoid sending the same cover letter to every employer.

Mention:

  • Company name
  • Position title
  • Relevant qualifications

Keep It Concise

A cover letter should typically be one page long.

Focus on Achievements

❌ Responsible for managing social media.

✔ Managed social media campaigns that increased engagement by 40%.

Show Enthusiasm

Employers want candidates who genuinely want the role.

Use Professional Language

Maintain a confident and professional tone throughout the letter.

Common Cover Letter Mistakes

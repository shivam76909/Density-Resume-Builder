What Is a CV?

A CV (Curriculum Vitae) is a detailed document that outlines your education, work experience, skills, certifications, achievements, publications, and professional accomplishments.

Unlike a resume, which is usually limited to one or two pages, a CV can be more comprehensive and include detailed information about your professional journey.

A CV is commonly used for:

  • Academic positions
  • Research roles
  • Government jobs
  • International applications
  • Scholarships
  • Fellowships
  • Professional careers

Essential Sections of a Professional CV

Contact Information

Include:

  • Full Name
  • Phone Number
  • Email Address
  • LinkedIn Profile
  • Portfolio Website

Professional Summary

Provide a brief overview of your qualifications.

Example:

Results-driven marketing professional with 5+ years of experience developing digital marketing strategies, managing campaigns, and improving brand visibility across multiple channels.

Education

Include:

  • Degree
  • Institution
  • Graduation Year
  • Academic Achievements

Example:

Bachelor of Technology in Computer Science

XYZ University  |  2026

Work Experience

List positions in reverse chronological order. Include:

  • Job Title
  • Company Name
  • Dates
  • Responsibilities
  • Achievements

Example:

Software Engineer  |  ABC Technologies  |  2023–Present

• Developed scalable web applications using React and Node.js

• Improved application performance by 30%

• Collaborated with cross-functional teams

Skills

  • Problem Solving

Certifications

Examples:

CV vs Resume: What's the Difference?

Feature CV Resume
Length Detailed Concise
Pages Multiple pages 1–2 pages
Purpose Academic & Professional Job Applications
Content Comprehensive Targeted
Customization Less frequent Tailored for each job

CV Writing Tips

Keep Information Organized

Use clear headings and consistent formatting.

Focus on Achievements

Instead of listing duties, highlight accomplishments.

❌ Responsible for sales.

✔ Increased sales revenue by 20% within six months.

Use Action Verbs

Examples:

  • Developed
  • Managed
  • Led
  • Designed
  • Implemented
  • Improved

Quantify Results

Numbers make achievements more impactful.

Examples:

  • Managed a team of 15 employees
  • Reduced costs by 25%
  • Increased customer satisfaction by 30%

Common CV Mistakes to Avoid

Avoid:

  • Spelling errors
  • Outdated information
  • Poor formatting
  • Generic summaries
  • Missing achievements
  • Unprofessional email addresses
  • Excessive personal information
